The web development industry in Kolkata has exploded. There are hundreds of agencies, freelancers, and "digital experts" competing for your business — and their quality varies enormously.

We're going to be unusually honest here: some of them will take your money and deliver something you're embarrassed to show people. Others will deliver a genuinely great website that grows your business.

The difference is mostly in the questions you ask before you start.

## Question 1: Can I see websites you've built for businesses like mine?

This is the most important question. Don't just look at a portfolio gallery — ask for live URLs of websites they've built for businesses in your industry.

If you're a restaurant, ask to see restaurant websites they've built. If you're a coaching institute, ask for institute websites.

Look at the sites on your phone. Check the loading speed. Click through the pages. Does it feel like a real, professional website — or a template with some colours changed?

**Red flag:** An agency that can't show you live, recent work.

## Question 2: Who exactly will be working on my website?

Many agencies sell the work and outsource the execution — sometimes to another city, sometimes to another country. The person you meet in the sales call may never touch your project.

Ask specifically:
- Who will design my website?
- Who will code it?
- Who is my point of contact for questions and updates?
- Will the same team handle post-launch support?

**Red flag:** Vague answers like "our team" with no specific names or roles.

## Question 3: What platform will you build it on, and why?

Your website platform matters enormously for speed, SEO, and long-term flexibility. Common options include:

- **WordPress** — flexible, widely used, good for content-heavy sites
- **Next.js / React** — fast, modern, excellent for performance and SEO
- **Wix / Squarespace** — quick to build, limited in flexibility and performance
- **Custom coded** — can be excellent or terrible, depending on the team

Ask them to explain their choice for your specific project. A good agency will give you a reasoned answer based on your needs — not just what they know best.

**Red flag:** "We build everything on Wix" with no further explanation, or agencies that can't articulate why they chose a specific platform.

## Question 4: What does the timeline look like, and what delays it?

Get a clear, written timeline with milestones — not just "4–6 weeks."

A proper timeline looks like:
- Week 1: Discovery and wireframe
- Week 2: Design mockup review
- Week 3–4: Development
- Week 5: Your review and feedback
- Week 6: Revisions and launch

Also ask: what happens if there are delays? What are the most common reasons projects run late? (Honest agencies will tell you — usually client feedback delays and scope changes.)

**Red flag:** Vague timelines with no milestones, or a refusal to put timelines in writing.

## Question 5: What's included in the price — and what costs extra?

This is where many businesses get burned. The quoted price covers the build, but then you discover:
- Domain registration costs extra
- Hosting costs extra
- SSL certificate costs extra
- Stock photos cost extra
- Email setup costs extra
- Any change after handover costs extra

Ask for an itemised breakdown of everything included. Then ask specifically: "What common things are NOT included in this price?"

**Red flag:** Reluctance to answer this question, or a vague "all-inclusive" claim without specifics.

## Question 6: Will my website be mobile-friendly and fast?

This should be table stakes in 2026 — but shockingly, many agencies still deliver slow, poorly-optimised websites.

Ask them:
- What Lighthouse score do your websites typically achieve?
- Can you show me a PageSpeed Insights result from a recent project?
- How do you handle image optimisation and Core Web Vitals?

A good website should load in under 3 seconds on mobile and score above 80 on Google's PageSpeed Insights. This directly affects your Google ranking.

**Red flag:** Agencies that don't know what PageSpeed Insights is, or dismiss the question.

## Question 7: What happens after launch?

Many agencies disappear after handover. You get your website and you're on your own. When something breaks or you need an update, you're suddenly hunting for someone who understands your codebase.

Ask:
- Do you offer a support period after launch? For how long?
- What does ongoing maintenance cost?
- If I need a small update 6 months from now, how do I request it and what does it cost?

**Red flag:** No structured post-launch support plan.

## Question 8: Can I have a reference from a previous client?

This is the ultimate trust signal. A confident agency will happily connect you with a past client who can speak honestly about their experience.

Ask for someone whose business is similar to yours, and actually call them. Ask: "Was the agency easy to work with? Did they deliver what they promised? Would you hire them again?"

**Red flag:** Any hesitation, excuses, or "our clients prefer privacy."
